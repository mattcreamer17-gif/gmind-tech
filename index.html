01 · The shift

GMind ships FinTech code. Trading platforms, brokerage systems, digital wealth, AWS, blockchain. Senior engineering hours for WealthCharts, ETNA Trader, Ultrade, OpticAsset. The work is real. It is also getting harder to price.

At Benzinga Fintech Day in December, Olga named the shift: AI tools now reach further in less time, which means the bill-of-materials on a fintech build is changing underneath everyone at once. The next unit of value sits above the code itself. It is the decision about which layer each problem belongs on.

02 · The frame

That is where Eduba lives. Jake runs Eduba. The frame is 60/30/10.

60% Traditional codeDatabases, APIs, plumbing. Postgres does the job.
30% Rule-based logicCompliance flows, state machines, pattern-matching.
10% Actual AIScoped, governed, auditable. The real work.

About 60 percent of what a fintech client asks for is traditional code and database work. About 30 percent is rule-based logic, compliance flows, state machines, and pattern-matching that never needed an LLM in the first place. The real AI work is the remaining 10 percent, and it needs to be scoped, governed, and audited. When the 60 and the 30 get dressed up as AI, budgets disappear and nobody ships.

The methodology Eduba publishes, Interpretable Context Methodology, gives a teachable structure for the 10 percent and a clean handoff to the 90.

03 · The fit

GMind and Eduba are a natural fit on the partnership side. Eduba already partners with NLP Logix, in machine learning since 2011 and running over 150 data scientists, for work that sits below the orchestration layer. GMind is structurally similar: senior fintech engineering, venture-studio flexibility, real client relationships in a regulated space.

A co-delivery arrangement would put Eduba’s orchestration audits and ICM implementation on top of GMind’s build work. It would also put a shared governance layer in front of fintech clients who will be asked to justify their AI posture to FINRA, SEC, and state regulators.

04 · Reading list

Two papers and a repo, in case the GMind team wants to pressure-test before the conversation.

  • Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM). Folder-structure-as-agent-architecture. Submitted to ACM TiiS. MIT license. The handoff story for a contractor-heavy delivery model.
